Acquiring Funds for Down Payment

Overview of Options

There are three potential options to acquire the necessary funds. These strategies are designed to avoid accessing your current investment accounts, leaving them in place for maximum earning potential.

1. Bridge Loan

A short-term loan that "bridges" the gap between buying a new home and selling your current one.

2. Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

A revolving line of credit based on your current home's equity, offering substantial cost savings.

3. Margin Loan

A loan secured against your own investment assets.

Funding Option 1: Bridge Loan

(Assuming a list price of ~$605,000 for current home)

  • Total Loan Amount: $158,570
  • Equity Unlock for New Home: $125,000
  • Maintenance Reserve: $18,150
  • Program Fee: 2.4% (~$14,500)
  • Inspection Fee: $900
  • Program Period: 120 days

Key Feature: No Payments

This unique loan requires no monthly payments. If your home is unsold after 120 days, it will be purchased to satisfy the loan. You receive the full difference (less costs) when it's sold to the final buyer.

Funding Option 2: Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

Primary Advantage

A HELOC offers "SUBSTANTIALLY less costs" when compared to a bridge loan.

  • Estimated Total Fees: ~$2,500
  • Required Payment: Interest-only (~$900/mo on $125k)

Features

  • No appraisal required
  • No full title package needed
  • Flexible timing and repayment

Funding Option 3: Margin Loan

This involves securing a loan against your own investment assets. Because it's secured by your portfolio, paying it back (plus interest) essentially adds back to your own net worth.

Recommendation

It is highly recommended to speak with your Financial Advisor (FA) to determine if this is an available and advantageous option for your specific financial situation.
